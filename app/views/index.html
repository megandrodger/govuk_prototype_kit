Firearm or shotgun licence

You can use these forms to:

  • apply for a firearm or shotgun licence
  • renew a firearm or shotgun licence
  • vary a firearm certificate

Information about medical arrangements is also available.

Prohibited weapons and aummunition (section 5) authority

You need a section 5 (S5) authority to handle prohibited weapons, component parts and ammunition.

You can use this service to:

  • apply for a new section 5 authority
  • renew or amend an existing section 5 authority

Shooting clubs can also use this service for section 7(3) weapons.

You will need the following:

  • the business’s name
  • the Companies House or registered charity number, if relevant
  • details of how the prohibited items will be obtained and used
  • documents justifying a business need for an S5 authority, for example contracts, invoices and letters of sponsorship
  • addresses of the business premises where the prohibited items will be stored
  • the types and quantities of prohibited items you want the authority to cover
  • the personal details of one or two named authority holders (home address, place and date of birth, and nationality)
  • contact details

Shooting club approval

Shooting clubs in England and Wales should use this service to apply to the Home Office:

  • for a target shooting club approval
  • to renew an existing target shooting club approval
  • to add new categories of weapons to an existing approval

Apply for, renew or amend a shooting club approval if you’re in England and Wales

Shooting clubs in Scotland should apply to the Scottish government.

Museum firearms licence

Museums in England and Wales can apply for this licence to:

  • apply for a new museum firearms licence
  • renew an existing museum firearm licence
  • add new exhibition or storage addresses to an existing licence
  • update contact details on an existing licence

If you exhibit or store prohibited weapons or ammunition you will need to apply for a separate section 5 weapons authority.

Who can apply

You can apply for this licence if you’re:

  • maintained by Parliament or local government
  • accredited by the Museum, Libraries and Archives Council (MLA) or Arts Council England and Wales

You can’t apply for this licence if you’re a non-accredited, privately funded or private collection. Instead you can apply for a firearm or shotgun certificate.
